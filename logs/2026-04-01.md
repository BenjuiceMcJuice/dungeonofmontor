## Key Decisions
- All game data in JSON, engine files pure logic — same shapes become Firestore docs later
- Common items named with Montor's disdain, special items are his personal collection
- Weapon types create stat-dependent playstyles (daggers→AGI, maces→STR, shields→DEF)
- Junk pile system activates 5+ dead stats in one mechanic
- Gifts found by searching junk, not from combat — rewards exploration over fighting
- Montor's mood randomised per run — prevents "learn the script" play
- Merchants are Montor's household staff, not random vendors
- CHA affects buy/sell prices
- "You remind me of her" is the emotional core hiding under sarcasm

## Design Notes — Endgame: Montor's Gifts & The Void Chamber

### Gift Progression (per-floor)
- One Montor's Gift per floor/level
- At the end of each floor, you interact with Montor to break/trade/unlock the Gift's power
- Powers are usable for the rest of the run AND persist across future runs
- Each Gift has a distinct power tied to its theme (body/mind/weapon/item categories from spec 12)

### The Void Chamber (final room)
- When you reach Montor's Void Chamber at the bottom of the dungeon, it's **sparse, minimalist, empty** — not the grand evil lair you'd expect
- The room is bare because Montor's Gifts are missing — scattered through the dungeon
- This is the reveal: the dungeon isn't hostile because Montor is evil, it's broken because his treasures are gone

### Endgame Loop
- **Goal**: complete runs bringing each Gift back to the Void Chamber and placing it there
- Each run you can carry one (or more?) Gifts down to restore
- Once ALL Gifts are restored to the chamber → game complete
- **Restored Gift bonus**: placing a Gift in the chamber permanently boosts that Gift's power — incentivises the restoration even beyond completion
- This gives **natural replayability** — you need multiple successful runs to restore everything
- Each run feels different because you're choosing which Gift to prioritise carrying down

### The Narrative Twist
- The Gifts are **sentimental family junk** — Gran's lottery ticket, Mum's rolling pin, etc.
- The *power* inside is secondary. Montor wants the tacky junk back because it's from his family.
- When you "break" a Gift, you extract the power (keep permanently) but the Gift becomes inert junk
- The junk is what you restore to the Void Chamber — Montor doesn't care about the power, he cares about his mum's stuff

### Terminals
- Each zone has a themed terminal where you apply extracted powers (Body/Mind/Weapon/Clothing)
- **Terminals are hidden under junk piles** — must search junk (PER rolls) to find them
- This ties junk pile system directly into Gift progression — PER becomes key stat
- **First run with a new Gift:** can't apply on same floor you found it — forces deeper
- **Subsequent runs:** power already extracted, any terminal works (including floor 1 Garden)
- Floors with 2 zones = 2 terminals = more choice/opportunity

### Why This Works
- Gives meaning to the Gift system beyond single-run power-ups
- The Void Chamber being empty is a narrative twist — expectation subversion
- "Restore the dungeon" is a more interesting goal than "kill the boss"
- The permanent power boost for restored Gifts means early restorations make later runs easier — natural difficulty curve
- Players who've beaten it once have a reason to go again with different Gift loadouts
- The "can't apply on same floor" rule creates natural forward momentum and makes 2-zone floors strategically valuable
