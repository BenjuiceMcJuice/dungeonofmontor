# Dungeon of Montor — Narrative Mode Demo

> A sample campaign session showing how the text-based RPG plays. One player, Montor as AI Dungeon Master (Estate Agent personality). Unedited from a live playtest.

---

## Character Creation

*A slow creak. Something ancient settling. The smell of damp earth and furniture polish.*

**MONTOR:** "Welcome, welcome! Come in, come in. Don't mind the door — it does that. Listed building, you understand. Grade II. Possibly Grade I, we're still waiting on the surveyor."

*Keith the Groundskeeper leans on a rake, eyeing you with suspicion. His stall has weapons, potions, basics.*

**MONTOR:** "Lovely little starter plot. South-facing. Good bones. The rats are a *feature*, not a problem. Character, I call it."

**You are GAZ — The Reluctant Knight.** Keith named you. You didn't argue. Sword, two health potions, and absolutely no ambitions.

---

## The Garden

*A garden gate. Iron bars. Moss. Something moved behind it.*

**MONTOR:** "Through there is my garden. There's a terminal somewhere — you'll need to find it to unlock the stairwell. There's also a boss. And rats. Lots of rats. Oh, and there's a gnome called Gerald somewhere in the junk. Don't. Touch. Gerald."

*The gate groans open. Beyond: cracked flagstones, overgrown hedges pressing in, and the faint sound of scratching.*

---

## The Tree

*An enormous oak. Too wide. Branches sprawling like arms mid-stretch. The bark is almost black but the leaves shimmer purple. Like oil on water.*

**MONTOR:** "You've noticed the centrepiece. Mature specimen. Been here longer than me. Longer than the house, actually. The house grew around *it*."

*He pauses.*

"I don't show everyone the tree, Gaz."

*Up close — the bark is carved. Dozens of scratches. Words in no language you recognise. There's a hollow at the base, something glinting inside. And wrapped around a low branch: a piece of cloth. Old. Embroidered. The letter M.*

**🎲 PER check (DC 11): Rolled 8. Failed.**

*You squint at the scratches. They're just scratches.*

**MONTOR** *(quickly)*: "Don't overthink it. It's a tree. People carve trees. Perfectly normal. South-facing, excellent root structure, low maintenance."

*He's talking too fast.*

---

## The Key

*You reach into the hollow. Cold inside. Your fingers close around something.*

**🎲 AGI check (DC 10): Rolled 14 + 1 = 15. Success.**

**Found: A small brass key.** Too small for a door. A jewellery box, maybe. Or a diary. Warm to the touch. Engraved with a flower — five petals.

**MONTOR:** "Put that back."

*The estate agent patter is gone. Just three words. Flat.*

"That's not part of the viewing. It's not for sale either. The key is — it's nothing. Decorative. Purely decorative. Lots of period properties have mysterious brass keys in ancient trees. It's in the brochure."

*He's lying. Even Gaz can tell.*

---

## The Rats

*Three shapes slink from the hedges. Rats. Not panicked — deliberate. Two small, one bigger. Missing an ear. They sit between you and the tree.*

*One-Ear stares at the key in your hand.*

**MONTOR:** "The garden watch. Don't mind them. They're not *mine* exactly. They just... live here. And they don't like people touching things."

**GAZ:** *Gets on his knees. Holds out the key. Apologises to the rats.*

**🎲 CHA check (DC 13): Rolled 11 - 1 = 10. Failed.**

*One-Ear isn't impressed. You're a trespasser holding stolen property.*

*But you didn't attack. You didn't run. You apologised to rats.*

**MONTOR** *(quiet, the estate agent gone entirely)*: "You're kneeling. In my garden. Apologising to my rats."

*A long pause.*

"Gran used to do that. Talk to them like they were people. She said they remembered things. I said she was mad."

*Another pause.*

"She wasn't mad."

*One-Ear looks up at wherever Montor's voice comes from. Squeaks twice. Softer. Then turns and leads the other rats back into the hedge.*

**The rats let you pass. You keep the key. One-Ear remembers you.**

---

## The Clearing

*A circular space. Flagstones in rings around a dry fountain. The stone basin is cracked, full of dead leaves.*

*The statue on top stops you.*

**A woman.** Stone. Life-sized. Holding a watering can, reaching out with the other hand, palm up. Like she's checking for rain. Her face is worn but she's smiling. Kind. Maternal.

At the base:

**"FOR MUM. WHO GREW EVERYTHING."**

**MONTOR:** "Don't read that."

*Too late.*

"It's just decorative. All good water features have inscriptions. Adds value."

*His voice is tight.*

There's a stone bench with a folded gardening apron. Faded green. Still has soil in the pockets. Someone laid it here carefully. Recently.

Behind the fountain — a small wooden door set into the wall. Low. No handle. Just a keyhole.

*A very small keyhole.*

*The brass key pulses warm.*

---

## The Potting Shed

*The key slides in. Perfect fit. Click. Gentle — like a locket opening.*

*Beyond: a potting shed. Wooden shelves. Clay pots stacked by size. Seed packets pinned to a board. A watering can. Gardening gloves laid out on the workbench, fingers pointing at the door.*

*Everything is clean. Not dusty-clean. Wiped-this-morning clean.*

*Someone maintains this room.*

**MONTOR:** "Get out."

*No personality. No patter. Just a voice.*

"That room isn't on the viewing."

*On the workbench: a framed photograph and a leather-bound notebook. The cover is embossed with flowers. Five petals.*

*Behind you, footsteps. Not rats. Something bigger. Slow. Like someone walking through their own garden.*

*One-Ear appears at the edge of the clearing. Watching. Not aggressive. Waiting.*

## What This Shows

**Narrative Mode** is not a text version of the roguelike. It's a different game built on the same world:

- **Montor IS the DM** — his personality colours every description, reaction, and consequence
- **Dice rolls matter but aren't everything** — failing the PER check meant Montor covered up, which was MORE interesting than succeeding
- **Choices have weight** — kneeling to the rats failed the CHA check but resonated with Montor's memories of his gran. The impression score went UP on a failed roll.
- **No UI buttons** — players type what they want to do. Anything. The AI handles it.
- **Mystery, not mechanics** — the brass key, the tree carvings, the letter M, the notebook, the mum connection. These are story threads that weave over a campaign, not items with stat bonuses.
- **Montor is vulnerable** — the estate agent mask slips when you touch things that matter. That's the real game: finding out who he is.

**Played asynchronously with friends,** each person takes actions when they can. Come back tomorrow and see what your friends did while you were away. Montor narrates everything. The campaign unfolds over days and weeks, like a group chat with a dungeon master who never sleeps and holds grudges.
